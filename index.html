About

Welcome to my Learning Digital Archaeology website! Here I will post assignments, critical reflections, and other insights regarding anthropology in the ‘digital age.’ As an anthropology student primarily interested in cultural anthropology, digital archaeology is somewhat of a foreign topic.

Here I will share the trials, tribulations, and triumphs I encountered while learning various technological skills relating to digital archaeology.

Enjoy

Background

A little about me. My name is Suzanne, or Sue. I am in the final semester of my undergraduate degree in anthropology. I am the co-chair of the anthropology student union at my university. Growing up, I always wanted to be a forensic anthropologist-thank you Bones, however inaccurate-until a professor explained how few forensic anthropologists there were in Canada, and that she was the consulting forensic anthropologist for the island. Needless to say, my hopes were slightly dashed. Until, I took a seminar in culture anthropology. Last spring, I completed the honours program, writing my essay on the perceptions of repatriation in anthropology literature.

I love museums. I’m fascinated by people and ‘culture.’ My main focus is community-based and ethnographic research (aka cultural anthropology). I took this class (Digital Archaeology: Virtual Heritage & Practice in the 21st Century) for a few reasons:

  1. I needed another method and theory class to graduate.
  2. It looked like a class that had real applicable job skills.
  3. I am interested in the ethics of digital archives, exhibits.
  4. My digital skills are(were?) lacking, to say the least.
  5. *Not a plug for grades* I heard the prof was great.
